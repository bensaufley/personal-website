I was largely entertained by this book, and it generally kept me guessing, which is most of what I want out of a thriller. A good thriller has enough "a-ha, I thought so" moments without letting you guess the whole thing, and this did well enough on that front, for me. I'm deducting points for the <Spoiler>woman lies about abuse for personal gain</Spoiler> trope, and for the fact that it got a little cartoonish at points.
